The bases

Traveling with Jonah

Three months old. First trip. Mountain country. The rules — for us.

RULE 01

Altitude — sleep below 8,000 ft

All seven bases pass. Day-trips up high are fine; come down to sleep.

Day-trip-only towns: Telluride (8,750), Mountain Village (9,500), Silverton (9,300), Pikes Peak summit, Independence Pass summit.

RULE 02

Watch for warning signs

Fussiness, poor feeding, fast or labored breathing, bluish lips.

Come down 1,000+ ft fast if any appear. Bailout to a lower base is always an option.

RULE 03

Carrier for trails, stroller for paths

Ergo / Tula carrier for any real trail. All-terrain stroller for Garden of the Gods, Animas River Trail, Riverwalks.

RULE 04

Hot springs — cooler pools only

Family pools at Mt. Princeton, Ouray, and Pagosa work. Skip the hottest terraces.

Confirm policies per property — most have age minimums for the hot pools.

RULE 05

Drive in 2–3h chunks

Real stop every ~90 min for feed/diaper. White noise + portable fan in the car. Sun shade on the rear window.

RULE 06

Layer for cold mornings

Late-May high-country mornings drop into the 30s F. Sun midday: 70s. Long sleeves & shade over sunscreen for under 6 mo.

RULE 07

Pack-and-play at every base

Verify with each Airbnb host before arrival. NOLA → Mountain Time is +1 hour — let his routine drift forward through the trip.

RULE 08

Bailouts are pre-planned

Hot springs not working? Skip and chill. Hike too steep? Turn around. The trip is built so any base can absorb a slow day.

Pre-trip checklist

  • Pediatrician visit — confirm 3-month altitude clearance + general travel advice
  • Acetaminophen dosing notes for the diaper bag
  • DM each Airbnb host about pack-and-play availability
  • Source pulse oximeter (Owlet sock or finger clip) before departure
  • Print insurance card + pediatrician contact for the glove box

BASE 04

Durango

Thu – Sat · May 28–30 6,500 ft · 3 nights
37.28°N · 107.88°W
Animas Valley Airbnb Hermosa Creek James Ranch Grill Animas River Trail

Lodging — rural-first

  • Animas Valley Airbnb — cabin or river-side house ~10 min north along the Animas River. Top pick for all 3 nights. River sounds, mountain views, drive into town for dinners. Search "Animas Valley" or "Hermosa" on Airbnb.
  • In-town alt: Rochester Hotel — historic boutique downtown, ~$280.
  • Splurge: General Palmer Hotel (Victorian).

Day 1 · Mesa Verde (Thu May 28)

  • Drive ~45m to Mesa Verde NP. Buy timed tickets in advance for Far View sites and Spruce Tree House overlook (no ladders, carrier-OK).
  • Skip ranger ladder tours (Cliff Palace, Balcony House) — not infant-compatible.
  • Lunch at Far View Lodge or pack one.
  • Lunch stop in Mancos (~900-person tiny town) at Olio or Absolute Bakery on the way back.

Day 2 · Hike + chill (Fri May 29)

  • Morning hike (carrier-friendly) — pick one:
    • Hermosa Creek Trail — ~10 min north, riverside, low-altitude, pick any segment.
    • Junction Creek / Colorado Trail start — gentle ascent, turn around at will.
    • Animas Mountain Trail — 3.5 mi loop in town, ~1,200 ft gain, big views.
  • Lunch at James Ranch Grill — working ranch with farm-to-table burgers, outdoor seating with mountain views.
  • Afternoon: chill at the Animas Valley Airbnb. River walk, porch nap, slow rural day.
  • Dinner: cook in or El Moro Tavern in town.

Day 3 · Town + train (Sat May 30)

  • Morning: Durango–Silverton Train, half-day to Cascade Canyon (~3.5h round trip — confirm off-season schedule). Optional — Million Dollar Hwy is now part of tomorrow's drive, so skip if it's too much.
  • Or walk/bike the Animas River Trail — paved, 7+ mi. Rentals at Pedal the Peaks.
  • Late lunch wandering Main Ave.
  • Pack up for tomorrow's scenic drive to Ridgway.

Dinners

El Moro Tavern, Ore House (steakhouse splurge), or Eolus (small plates).

→
Durango to Ridgway via Million Dollar Highway · ~3h · 110 mi · The iconic San Juans drive — the move IS the experience. US-550 north → Coal Bank Pass (10,640 ft) → Molas Pass (10,910 ft, stunning views) → Silverton (lunch at Avalanche Brewing, walk Greene Street; 9,300 ft so don't linger) → Red Mountain Pass (11,018 ft, the famous mining-country hairpins) → Ouray (Switzerland of America) → Ridgway. Eliminates the need for a separate Million Dollar Hwy day from Ridgway. Leave Durango by 9, lunch in Silverton, arrive Ridgway mid-afternoon.
BASE 05

Ridgway / Ouray

Sun – Tue · May 31 – Jun 2 7,000–7,800 ft · 3 nights
38.15°N · 107.75°W
Ridgway cabin + Beaumont Bear Creek Trail Yankee Boy Basin Ouray Hot Springs Bon Ton

Lodging — split the 3 nights

  • Nights 1–2 (May 31, Jun 1): Airbnb cabin in Ridgway (Hartwell Park or N of town with mountain views) — ~$200/night.
  • Night 3 (Jun 2): Beaumont Hotel, Ouray — Victorian splurge, ~$320.

Day 1 · Settle + Ouray Hot Springs (Sun May 31)

  • Arrive after the Million Dollar Hwy drive. Decompress.
  • Walk Ridgway downtown — Cimarron Books, True Grit Café (yes, that True Grit).
  • Late afternoon: Ouray Hot Springs Pool (10m south) — shallow family sections.
  • Dinner: Maggie's Kitchen (Ouray) or The Adobe Inn (Ridgway).

Day 2 · Telluride day trip — the Dolomites moment (Mon Jun 1)

  • Drive ~45m over Dallas Divide (one of the most photographed views in CO) to Telluride.
  • Ride the free gondola Telluride ↔ Mountain Village.
  • Lunch options: Allred's at the gondola top (call to confirm off-season), Brown Dog Pizza, 221 South Oak, or There.
  • Walk Bear Creek Trail or Cornet Falls (short, dramatic) from town.
  • Optional drive partway to Bridal Veil Falls.
  • Back to Ridgway for low-altitude sleep.

Day 3 · Hike + Black Canyon + move to Beaumont (Tue Jun 2)

Combined final day. Morning hike, afternoon Black Canyon, evening Beaumont check-in.

Morning hike (pick one, carrier-friendly):

  • Box Canyon + Cascade Falls combo (Ouray) — quick wins, both 5-min walks, dramatic. Best low-effort option.
  • Twin Peaks Trail (Ouray) — moderate, ~3 mi RT, ~1,000 ft gain, big views back over town.
  • Yankee Boy Basin — drive a 4WD/AWD road up from Ouray into a wildflower bowl; easy meadow walks at the top.

Sam solo dawn option: Blue Lakes Trail — out by 6am, back by 11. ~7 mi RT, 1,800 ft gain. Gigi + Jonah do something easy meanwhile.

Afternoon — Black Canyon of the Gunnison south rim:

  • Drive ~50m to the rim. Drive the rim road, short stroller-OK overlooks (Pulpit Rock, Sunset View, Painted Wall). Picnic lunch on the rim.
  • Optional: Owl Creek Pass Road dirt drive on the way back — gravel, ranchland and aspens, almost zero traffic.

Evening: Move to Beaumont Hotel in Ouray for a Victorian-splurge final night. Dinner: Bon Ton at the St Elmo Hotel.

Road bike

Ridgway → Colona → back, or the Uncompahgre Riverway path. Quiet shoulders early. Slot it on Day 1 evening or Day 3 morning.

BASE 06

Palisade

Wed – Thu · Jun 3–4 4,720 ft · 2 nights
39.11°N · 108.35°W
Wine Country Inn Colorado National Monument Bin 707 Pool weather

Why the warmth

After 10 days of 50–70°F mountain mornings, Palisade is the deliberate weather break. Expect 85–90°F afternoons. Pool weather. Patio-dinner weather. T-shirts for Jonah for the first time.

Lodging

  • Wine Country Inn — vineyard hotel with pool, ~$250–300. Top pick for hot weather with a baby.
  • Spoke and Vine Motor Lodge — boutique retro motel in town, walkable to tasting rooms.
  • Vineyard cabin Airbnb — search "Palisade" for orchard cottages.

Day 1 · Arrive + Colorado National Monument (Wed Jun 3)

  • Arrive Palisade mid-afternoon. Check in, pool, recover from drive.
  • Late afternoon: drive 15 min to Colorado National Monument. Take Rim Rock Drive at golden hour — 23 miles of red-rock canyon overlooks (Independence Monument, Wedding Canyon, Book Cliffs). Stay in the car between viewpoints; Jonah in carrier or stroller for short walks.
  • Dinner: Pêche at Hotel Maverick (Grand Junction, 15m west) or Bin 707 for a wine-bar-style menu.

Day 2 · Wine + peach country (Thu Jun 4)

  • Morning: East Orchard Mesa Road tasting tour. Visit Carlson Vineyards, Whitewater Hill, Restoration Vineyards — tasting rooms are AC, spit don't drink with baby.
  • Stop at fruit stands: Talbott's Mountain Gold, Clark Family Orchards. Cherries ripen in early June; peaches start in July. Bring a cooler.
  • Lunch: patio at a winery (try Sauvage Spectrum or Talon), or Inari's for sushi in Palisade.
  • Afternoon: pool, or Riverbend Park along the Colorado River for a paved stroller walk.
  • Dinner: vineyard tasting menu or back to GJ for Bin 707.
→
Palisade to Redstone via Grand Mesa Scenic Byway · Fri Jun 5 · 3h 30m · 130 mi · The scenic alternative to I-70. Brief I-70 east → exit 49 → Hwy 65 South (Grand Mesa Scenic Byway) → up and over Grand Mesa (world's largest flat-top mountain, 300+ alpine lakes, summit 10,839 ft — day altitude only) → down to Cedaredge → Hwy 92 east through Hotchkiss + Paonia → Hwy 133 north over McClure Pass → Redstone. Stops: Grand Mesa lake pull-offs; lunch in Paonia at Living Farm Café or Revolution Brewing.

BASE 08

Carbondale

Sat – Sun · Jun 6–7 6,180 ft · 2 nights
39.40°N · 107.21°W
Sopris-view Airbnb Maroon Bells dawn ride Hay Park Trail Penny Hot Springs Pine Creek Cookhouse

Lodging

  • Airbnb in town with a Mt. Sopris view — the entire reason we're here. Top pick.
  • Distillery Inn at Marble Distilling — boutique, ~$250.

Day 1 · Settle in (Sat Jun 6, after arriving from Redstone)

  • Coffee at Bonfire.
  • Easy hike: Hay Park Trail (Sopris views, lower elevation) — turn around as needed.
  • Road bike: Rio Grande Trail Carbondale → Basalt — paved, mostly flat, valley views.
  • Penny Hot Springs at sunset — free, riverside, 15m south on Hwy 133.
  • Dinner: Town (modern American), Allegria (Italian), Phat Thai, or Free Range Kitchen.

Day 2 · Aspen + Maroon Bells day trip (Sun Jun 7)

  • Dawn bike to Maroon Bells (Sam): drive 35m to Aspen, ride from Aspen Highlands lot up Maroon Creek Road. ~5 mi each way, paved, ~1,200 ft of climb (8,200 → 9,580 ft). Road is closed to cars before 8am — empty, silent, alpenglow on the Bells. Bucket-list CO road-bike experience. ~2h round trip. Rentals: Hub of Aspen or Aspen Velo — reserve a road bike the day before.
  • Meanwhile: Gigi + Jonah sleep in. Optional later Maroon Bells shuttle (recreation.gov, opens ~30 days out) up to the lake to meet you in Aspen, or breakfast in town.
  • Late morning: regroup, breakfast at Bonnie's or Spring Café.
  • Lunch options: Woody Creek Tavern in Woody Creek (~250-person hamlet, Hunter S. Thompson's old hangout, kitschy roadhouse with a ridiculous patio), or Meat & Cheese, White House Tavern, or Hooch in Aspen.
  • Afternoon: drive Castle Creek Road to Ashcroft Ghost Town (30m south of Aspen, free) — preserved 1880s mining village in a meadow with the Elk Range behind it.
  • Wander Aspen, ride the Silver Queen Gondola if running, cocktails at the J-Bar at Hotel Jerome (CO institution since 1889).
  • Drive 35m back to Carbondale to sleep low.
  • Dinner: back in Carbondale at Town or Allegria, or splurge at Pine Creek Cookhouse in Ashcroft on the way back (reserve weeks ahead).
