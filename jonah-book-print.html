Their offerings include 16-page and 24-page board books, so your 11-page layout will be short. Options: repeat the cover, add a dedication page, or call them at (716) 995-3108 to ask about custom counts. They also accept emailed files at help@pintsizeproductions.com.

To export:

  1. Click Save as PDF below (or use your browser's Print → Save as PDF).
  2. In the print dialog: enable "Background graphics" (Chrome) or "Print backgrounds" (Safari) — the black pages will print white without it.
  3. Set margins to "None" and scale to 100%.
  4. Confirm paper size is 7.25 × 7.25 in custom — or any "fit" option that doesn't crop.
  5. Upload the resulting PDF to Pint Size Productions (or any board-book printer).

Pint Size note: their standard book may require a minimum number of pages (often 12 or 16). If yours requires more than 9, you can repeat the cover page, add a blank "for Jonah" inscription page at the start, or repeat the goodnight page as a back-inside cover. Confirm their template requirements directly — they sometimes provide a downloadable PSD/PDF template that overrides these defaults.

For Jonah

A book about your first time in the mountains
Seven places · May–June 2026
Our tripColorado
The map.

From the airport, up to the mountains and back. Seven little places, all in a row.

Jonah was at every one.

Base 01Colorado Springs
A hawk!

She landed on Mommy's glove while you took a nap. Her wings were wide. Her eyes were sharp.

Mommy told you all about her.

Base 02Salida
The river.

Whoosh, whoosh — the water ran cold and fast right through the middle of town.

Jonah watched the rafts go by.

Base 03Pagosa Springs
Splash!

The hot springs were warm and steamy. Mommy dipped Jonah's little toes in the water.

Jonah smiled.

Base 04Durango
Choo-choo.

The big black train rolled slowly down the track, puffing white smoke into the tall pine trees.

Jonah heard the whistle blow.

Base 05Ridgway & Ouray
Mountains.

Tall, tall mountains stood all around. Snow on top. Green below. Big blue sky above.

Jonah looked up, and up, and up.

Base 06Redstone
A horse.

She stood quiet in the meadow. Her tail went swish, swish. Her ears went up at the wind.

Jonah heard her hoof go tap.

Base 07Carbondale
Round and round.

Daddy's bike spun up the empty mountain road. The sky turned pink behind the peaks.

Jonah waited for Daddy to come home.
