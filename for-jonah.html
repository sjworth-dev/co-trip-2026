A note for later

For Jonah

A small record of your first time in the mountains
Colorado · May 24 – June 8, 2026

Hi, kid.

You were three months old, and we took you to the mountains.

You won't remember any of it. We took you anyway because we wanted you with us, and because we wanted to look back someday — at this, at you — and say yes, you were there. From the beginning.

You were good in the car. You napped through the high passes. You watched the alpenglow paint the Maroon Bells from your stroller while Dad rode his bike up the empty road at sunrise. Mommy met a Harris's hawk on the very first morning — you were with a sitter, and she told you all about it. You let strangers tell you you were beautiful. You let us hold you a lot.

Some things from the trip — for you to know later:

Some of your firsts

  1. First Rocky Mountains — Pikes Peak from Colorado Springs
  2. First grand hotel — the Broadmoor, with falconry on the lawn
  3. First mountain road — Colorado Springs to Salida, US-24 west
  4. First hot spring — the riverside terraces at Pagosa
  5. First national park — Mesa Verde
  6. First mountain pass — Coal Bank, Molas, Red Mountain (you slept through them all)
  7. First gondola — Telluride to Mountain Village
  8. First ghost town — Ashcroft, in a meadow with the Elk Range behind it
  9. First time at twelve thousand feet — Independence Pass, on the way back to Denver
